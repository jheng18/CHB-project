Crime Data Analysis

Below is the data analysis

Count for Crime by month, 2013-2017:

Each box refers to number of crimes happened within a month in a certain year. Overall, occurrences of crimes are pretty even cross month and cross year, with small peaks from summer to fall.

Data Source: City of Chicago, Data Portal.

Count for Crime by primary crime type, 2013-2017:

Each box refers to number of crimes happened belong to a crime type in a certain year. Overall, more crimes happened in Hyde Park were theft, battery, and criminal damage. Note: Crimes number <= 2 were removed from the graph. ‘Vio’ stands for Violence.

Data Source: City of Chicago, Data Portal.

Count of Crime by aggregate crime type, 2013–2017:

Based on the damage level for humans of a certain crime, crime types are categorized into five aggregate types.
Damage Level 1: Deceptive practice, Interference with pubic officer, Liquor law violation, Public peace violation;
Damage Level 2: Concealed carry license violation, Narcotics, Theft, Stalking;
Damage Level 3: Criminal damage, Intimidation, Motor Vehicle theft, Obscenity, Public indecency ;
Damage Level 4: Assault, Battery, Burglary, Robbery, Weapons violation;
Damage Level 5: Criminal sexual assault, Homicide, Human trafficking, Kidnapping, Murder, Offense involving children, Sex offense
Each box refers to number of crimes happened belong to an aggregate crime type in a certain year. Overall, more crimes happened in Hyde Park were crime type 2 and crime type 4 crimes.

Data Source: City of Chicago, Data Portal

Count for Crime by Time Slot, 2013–2017:

A day is divided into 12 timeslots. Each bar refers to the total number of crimes happened within a timeslot from 2013-2017. Overall, more crimes happened from noon to midnight.

Data Source: City of Chicago, Data Portal
